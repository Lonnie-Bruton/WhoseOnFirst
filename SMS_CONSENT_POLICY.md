## Purpose

This document outlines the SMS notification consent and opt-out procedures for the WhoseOnFirst on-call rotation management system. This system sends automated SMS notifications to team members when their on-call shift begins.

---

## SMS Notification Service Description

WhoseOnFirst is an automated on-call rotation and notification system that:
- Sends SMS notifications to team members when their scheduled on-call shift begins
- Delivers notifications daily at 8:00 AM Central Time (America/Chicago)
- Provides shift duration and end time information
- Is used exclusively for internal team on-call schedule coordination

**Message Frequency:** Maximum of 7 messages per week (one per scheduled shift)

**Message Type:** Operational notifications for work-related on-call duty assignments

**Sample Message:**
> "WhoseOnFirst: [Your Name], your on-call shift has started. Duration: 24h (until [Day] [Time]). Questions? Contact admin."

## Consent to Receive SMS Notifications

By providing your mobile phone number to the WhoseOnFirst system, you:

2. **Acknowledge** that message and data rates may apply based on your mobile carrier plan
3. **Understand** that notifications are sent via Twilio's messaging service

## How to Opt-In

To receive SMS notifications from WhoseOnFirst:

1. **Provide Your Phone Number:** Submit your mobile phone number (in E.164 format: +1XXXXXXXXXX) to the system administrator
2. **Verbal Consent:** Provide verbal consent to Lonnie Bruton (lbruton@lonniebruton.com) that you wish to receive on-call shift notifications
3. **Email Consent:** Send an email to lbruton@lonniebruton.com with:
   - Your full name
   - Your mobile phone number
   - Subject line: "SMS Opt-In for WhoseOnFirst"
   - Message: "I consent to receive on-call shift notifications via SMS at [your phone number]"

**Opt-in is voluntary and can be revoked at any time** using the opt-out procedures below.

---

## How to Opt-Out (STOP)

You may opt-out of receiving SMS notifications from WhoseOnFirst at any time using any of the following methods:

### Method 1: Reply STOP
Reply **STOP** to any SMS notification you receive from the WhoseOnFirst system. This will immediately unsubscribe your phone number from future notifications.

### Method 2: Email Opt-Out
Send an email to **lbruton@lonniebruton.com** with:
- **Subject:** "SMS Opt-Out for WhoseOnFirst"
- **Body:** Include your full name and mobile phone number
- **Example:** "Please remove my phone number +1XXXXXXXXXX from WhoseOnFirst SMS notifications"

### Method 3: Verbal Opt-Out
Contact Lonnie Bruton directly (lbruton@lonniebruton.com) via:
- **Email:** Request opt-out verbally during any communication
- **In-Person:** Notify the system administrator verbally of your wish to opt-out

**Processing Time:** Opt-out requests will be processed within 24 hours. You may receive one additional message if a notification is already queued for delivery.

## Privacy and Data Protection

### Information We Collect
- Mobile phone number (E.164 format)
- Full name (for notification personalization)
- On-call shift schedule assignments

### How We Use Your Information
- To send automated SMS notifications for scheduled on-call shifts
- To maintain on-call rotation records
- To comply with operational and audit requirements

### Information Sharing
- Your phone number is shared **only** with Twilio (our SMS service provider) for message delivery
- We **do not sell, rent, or share** your phone number with third parties for marketing purposes
- Your information is used exclusively for operational on-call notifications

### Data Retention
- Phone numbers are retained for the duration of your participation in the on-call rotation
- Upon opt-out, your phone number is deactivated but retained in logs for audit purposes
- Notification logs are retained for 90 days for troubleshooting and compliance

### Security
- Phone numbers are stored securely in an encrypted database
- Access is restricted to authorized system administrators only
- Twilio's enterprise-grade security protects message delivery
